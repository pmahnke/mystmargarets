The Week the War Broke Out

The Great War started twice. The first time was on Tuesday 28th July 1914 when, following the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria by a Serbian nationalist, Austria-Hungary declared war against Serbia. The second time was a
week later, on Tuesday 4th August 1914, when Britain declared war against Germany after their refusal to respect the neutrality of Belgium.

My St Margarets Magazine - Spring 'Jubilee' 2012 Edition

Hello and welcome to this rather special edition of My St Margarets magazine.

For me, featured in this edition, are three must do visits - The Richmond Museum, Orleans Gallery and the Turner House. The twice yearly Fairs are so much fun and a good chance to catch up with friends and neighbours. This year’s Jubilee Fair in Crown Road on Sunday 3rd June sounds absolutely amazing. The fair is the traditional street fair that we all love and also a Jubilee celebration party. What could be better than enjoying the live music, street entertainers, the fun and games and then sitting down to a nice Jubilee tea with friends and neighbours!

Highlights in this edition include Martyn Day’s fascinating article about Queen Victoria’s Diamond Jubilee and I was really taken by Chas’s article on antiques, I am amazed at the prices being fetched by some of the Titanic memorabilia. The centre pages feature a recent shopping trip made by a very special visitor who seems to have thoroughly enjoyed her visit to St Margarets - with so many independent specialist shops everyone is at home in St Margarets.

My St Margarets Magazine - Autumn 2011 Edition

The autumn edition of the My St Margarets Magazine is about to be published - look out for your copy arriving through local letter boxes over the next week. Copies will also be available online and from local businesses.

Local historian Martyn Day takes us on a stroll down Montpelier Row and shares the streets literary past with us. The Food-Phile has some wonderful recipes for you to try and Armstrong’s Family Butchers celebrate their 4th year anniversary in St Margarets and remind us of all the delicious stews and casseroles that we can make that can help with the cold winter months approaching. Mr Brocklehurst has the pleasure of meeting Greg Dyke and we hear from The Fox Family who once again share with us their summer holiday antics.
